This is an item in the Slurry Infrastructure grant.

Grant contribution

£5,635 each

Grant contribution

£31.50 per m3

Grant scheme information

You can apply for a Slurry Infrastructure grant to improve or expand your slurry storage capacity and to cover your stores.

  • replace existing stores that are no longer fit for purpose
  • add additional storage, if other stores that will stay part of your system meet slurry storage regulations, were built to relevant standards and remain fit for purpose
  • expand an existing store, for example by adding another ring to a suitable steel tank
  • cover new and existing stores

Eligible equipment must go towards a project that meets at least one of these criteria.

You must use the grant to reach the grant storage requirements of:

  • 6 months for beef and dairy cattle
  • 8 months for pigs

Specification

Fixed electrically powered centrifugal chopper pump with minimum 80 millimetres (mm) diameter outlet. Minimum transfer rate of 100m³ an hour at a 10m pumping head.

Pump to be suitable for operation in environments with pH down to 5.5.

  • silage, slurry and agricultural fuel oil (SSAFO) 2010 regulations
  • BS5502-50
  • BS EN ISO 28722:2011

The store must have a minimum freeboard of 300 millimetres (mm) and be designed with a lifespan of more than 20 years.

Reference cost includes

The reference cost includes:

  • supply and installation
  • electrical connection to farms distribution board

Mobile pumps are not eligible under this item.

Further information

Read Slurry Infrastructure grant: guidance for applicants to find out more information about slurry storage including how to apply.
