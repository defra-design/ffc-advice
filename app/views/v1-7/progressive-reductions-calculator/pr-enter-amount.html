How payments are calculated

The calculator will estimate your payments for each year based on the starting payment amount you add.

The value of this amount should be taken from your 2020 BPS claim statement, or your most recent claim statement, depending on your circumstances.

You should exclude payments for land in Scotland, Wales or Northern Ireland from your starting payment amount.

If you claimed BPS in 2020

You should use the total of the following amounts shown on your 2020 BPS claim statement as your starting payment amount:

  • BPS 'gross value' amount
  • young farmer claim value, if relevant

This is the value of your claim before the RPA applied:

  • any penalties
  • a 5% reduction if your claim was more than €150,000

If you claimed BPS for the first time after 2020

You should use the total of the following amounts shown on your most recent BPS claim statement as your starting payment amount:

  • BPS 'gross value' amount
  • young farmer claim value, if relevant

2024 payment results

The calculator assumes you received the same claim value each year between 2022-2022 to calculate the 2024 payment result.

To get a more accurate result for 2024 if you have not claimed BPS each year or your land has changed, you can either:

  • use the average claim value you received between 2020-2022 as your starting payment amount
  • calculate what proportion of the the 2024 payment you’re likely to receive

You should divide the 2024 payment:

  • by 3 if you claimed for 1 out of the 3 years
  • by 3, and multiply by 2 if you claimed for 2 out of the 3 years
