Check how your BPS payments will reduce until 2024

Payments from the Basic Payment Scheme (BPS) will reduce each year from 2021 until the end of 2023 in England.

Delinked payments should replace BPS payments from 2024, and will reduce each year until payments finish in 2027. The Department for Environment, Food & Rural Affairs (Defra) and Rural Payments Agency (RPA) will provide more details about what's happening after 2024 when they're available.

Use the calculator to get an estimate of how much money you'll receive each year as payments reduce.

The calculator should give you a result in less than 2 minutes.

You do not need to add personal information to the calculator. No personal information is collected or stored in the calculator.

There is no limit to the number of times you can use the calculator.

How my payment reductions are calculated

The following percentage reductions apply to:

  • BPS payments between 2021 and 2023
  • delinked payments in 2024
Progressive reductions calculation
Scheme year 2021 2022 2023 2024
£30,000.00 or less 5% 20% 35% 50%
Amounts above £30,000.00 and no more than £50,000.00 10% 25% 40% 55%
Amounts above £50,000.00 and no more than £150,000.00 20% 35% 50% 65%
Amounts above £150,000.00 25% 40% 55% 70%

Before you start

Add your 'starting payment amount' to the calculator.

Your starting payment amount is the total BPS payment you're owed in each scheme year (including any young farmer payment), after the RPA applies all other reductions and penalties.

The reductions apply to BPS and delinked payments in England. You should exclude payments for land in Scotland, Wales or Northern Ireland from your starting payment amount.

Read more information about how BPS payments are calculated in 2022.

In 2024, your starting payment amount for delinked payments will be a reference amount that's based on the average BPS payments you received between 2020 and 2022. Find out how delinked payments will be calculated.
