Guidance

Funding for farmers and land managers

You can apply for money to improve the environment and your farm's productivity if you're a farmer, land manager or forester.

From:
Department for Environment, Food & Rural Affairs and Rural Payments Agency
Published
9 March 2022
Last updated
7 June 2022 + show all updates

Applies to England

  • Contents
  • One-off payments
  • Ongoing payments
  • Get advice

You can apply for any of the schemes in this guidance, even if you get paid by the Basic Payment Scheme (BPS). You'll need to check you're eligible for each scheme before you apply.

You can be in more than one scheme at a time, but you cannot be paid twice for the same activity.

One-of payments

One-off payments are for activities that you do once or to help you buy equipment or other items.

Investing in equipment, technology and infrastructure

The Farming Investment Fund (FIF) provides funding for equipment, technology, and infrastructure that improves farm productivity and benefits the environment.

If you're a pig, beef or dairy farmer whose farming systems produce slurry, you'll be able to apply for a grant from autumn 2022 to help:

  • build new slurry stores
  • replace existing slurry stores
  • expand existing slurry stores

These stores will provide 6 months of storage. You can apply for a grant between £25,000 and £250,000.

The other grants within FIF are currently closed to new applicants.

Research and innovation

You can apply for grants for innovation, research and development from the Farming Innovation Programme.

Improving animal health and welfare

The Animal Health and Welfare Pathway provides money to help improve the health and welfare of animals.

Improving the health of trees affected by disease

You can apply for money to improve tree health through the tree health pilot scheme if you manage certain trees or woodlands affected by specific pests and diseases in some regions of England.

Supporting national parks and areas of outstanding natural beauty (AONBs)

The Farming in Protected Landscapes Programme provides funding to all farmers and land managers within an AONB or National Park in England who are planning projects that:

  • support nature recovery
  • mitigate climate change
  • provide opportunities for people to discover nature
  • protect or improve the quality and character of the landscape

The programme will run until March 2024.

Restoring peatlands

The Nature for Climate Peatland Grant Scheme (NCPGS) provides money to restore peatlands in the uplands and lowlands of England. It's a competitive grant scheme that'll run until 2025.

Leaving farming

The Lump Sum Exit Scheme is for farmers in England who receive BPS and wish to leave farming.

Once you receive a lump sum payment, you will not be eligible for any further BPS payments or delinked payments.

You will also not be eligible to enter into new agreements under the following schemes unless you repay the lump sum first:

  • Sustainable Farming Incentive (SFI)
  • some options under Countryside Stewardship
  • some options under Local Nature Recovery (LNR)

Ongoing payments

Ongoing payments are for activities to produce environmental outcomes that usually take you a year or more to complete.

BPS

You can apply for money from the BPS each year if you carry out certain farming or environmental activities on your land.

BPS payments are reducing every year until the end of 2023. From 2024, 'delinked' payments will replace BPS and will reduce each year until payments finish in 2027.

You can get an estimate of how much money you'll receive each year.

Improving the health of soils

In 2022, farmers who are eligible for BPS can apply for money from SFI to improve soil health.

Understanding moorland

In 2022, farmers who are eligible for BPS can apply for money from SFI to identify and assess the environmental features of their moorland.

Managing habitats, woodlands, flood risk and reducing water pollution

You can apply for Countryside Stewardship (CS) to get paid to look after and improve the environment. Applications are open until 2023 (for agreements starting in 2024).

Environmental Stewardship (ES) agreements are not open to new applicants. If you have an existing ES agreement you can switch to CS, SFI and LNR agreements from 1 January 2023 without penalty.

Making space for nature on farms and in the countryside

You'll be able to apply for money from LNR to get paid for things that help the local environment. For example:

  • managing and restoring habitats
  • planting trees and managing woodlands
  • restoring peatlands
  • using natural flood management techniques

Defra will trial LNR with land managers from 2023. All eligible applicants can apply from the end of 2024.

Large scale landscape and ecosystem restoration projects

Money for Landscape Recovery will be available to support wildlife and river projects such as:

  • creating and expanding nature reserves
  • creating woodland
  • restoring peatland and wetland

Applications for the first round of projects have closed. Defra will invite applications for the next round in 2023.

Planting trees and managing woodland

Until 2024, you can get paid to plant trees and create woodland through the England Woodland Creation Offer on areas that are at least 1 hectare.

There are other grants available for woodland creation, maintenance, management and tree health.

  • England's Community Forests - funding is available for planting trees in some areas
  • The Northern Forest - you can get paid to create woodland in parts of Lancashire, Merseyside, Yorkshire, Derbyshire, Nottinghamshire and Lincolnshire
  • The National Forest - money is available to create and maintain forests across the country

Joining farming

The New Entrant Support Scheme aims to encourage new starters into farming. Defra plans to trial this scheme from 2022 to 2023.

Get advice

You can contact:

  • the Defra Rural Service about funding, including BPS, Countryside Stewardship and Common Agricultural Policy (CAP)
  • the Farming Advice Service about cross compliance, BPS, pesticide use, water use and quality

You can also get support from:

  • organisations that provide advice through the Future Farming Resilience Fund to help you plan for the future - this advice is free if you get BPS
  • the Farming Community Network about arable farming, animal health and welfare
  • Woodland Creation officers about planting and managing woodland
  • your Catchment Sensitive Farming local farm advisor for advice about water or air quality, or reducing flood risk on your land
  • the Bovine TB Advisory Service to help reduce the incidence and severity of bovine TB breakouts
